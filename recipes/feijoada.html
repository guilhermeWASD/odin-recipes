Feijoada

Description

Wednesday and Saturday are consecrated Feijoada days for Brazilians! Its origin is still much debated, but it is assumed that the dish is an adaptation of the Portuguese stew, since this type of recipe was very common in Europe (such as Puchero in Spain and Cassoulet in France). The Brazilian version is made with black beans, various cuts of pork and beef, onion and garlic, and is “religiously” accompanied by rice, sautéed cabbage, farofa, crackling, orange and vinaigrette.

Ingredients (20 servings)
  • 1kg of black beans
  • 100g of dried meat
  • 70g pork ear
  • 70g pork tail
  • 70g pork feet
  • 100g of pork ribs
  • 50g of pork loin
  • 100g of papaya/li>
  • 150g of sausage
Seasoning
  • 2 large chopped onions
  • 1 bunch of minced green onion
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 1 or 2 oranges
  • 40 ml of drip
  • salt if you need
Steps
  1. Soak the meats for 36 hours or more, change the water several times, if it is hot or summer, place ice on top or in cold layers.
  2. Put it to cook step by step: the hard meats, then the soft meats.
  3. When it's soft, put the beans, and remove the meats.
  4. Finally season the beans.
  5. accompaniments: Cabbage, white rice, orange, chops, farofa, pumpkin kibbeh, baião de dois, bacon, crackling, sausage and seasoned broth - cups.
